# Paper 3 — Literature Review Execution Plan (Revised)

**Objective:** Read enough to draft Section 2 by May 2, 2026 (10 days from Apr 13).
**Total reading budget:** ~18-19 hours reading + 4-6 hours outlining/drafting.
**Rule:** Every paper read should serve a specific sentence or paragraph in Section 2.

**Organizing principle:** What depth does each paper need given its role in your argument and what you already know?

Your prior knowledge matters for tiering. You're not a cold reader on most of this:
- **Paper 1 (Frontiers in Climate)** → you already command the I-O employment methodology
- **RAND background** → you already know DMDU/RDM/PRIM operationally
- **Weekly meetings with Mealy/Bucker group** → you have familiarity with their conceptual arguments

That doesn't mean you skip those papers — it means you read them differently. A committee won't ask "do you know what I-O is?" They'll ask "how exactly does your approach differ from Bucker et al.?" and "what specific gap in the Hanna et al. review does your paper fill?"
